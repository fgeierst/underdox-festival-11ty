THE WASH

Lee Lynch & Lee Anne Schmitt | USA 2005
20 min.

Format: Beta SP
Material: MiniDV and Super8
Original language : English

Script: Lee Lynch, Lee Anne Schmitt
Camera: Lee Lynch, Lee Anne Schmitt
Editing: Lee Lynch, Lee Anne Schmitt

Sound: Lee Lynch, Lee Anne Schmitt
Music: Aaron Hemphil

Production: Lee Lynch & Lee Anne Schmitt

Special Mention, Marseille 2006

German Premiere

 

The Wash is a portrait of the river “Wash” that runs behind the older part of Newhall, California, where the filmmaker used to live.  They shot the “Wash” on Super8 film and then finished it on video. It is a collaboration between them, describing the ways the “Wash” is used, and the people who use it, themselves included. It charts the way this land has changed since they began developing Newhall and the surrounding community of Valencia for housing, a development that is expected to bring over 250,000 more people into the area by the year 2015.


Lee Lynch, born in 1980 in Dallas, has been making films from the age of 13. He studied at the California Institute of Arts.

Lee Anne Schmitt, born in Chicago. She studied at the Northwestern University and works a a filmmaker, photographer and performer. Her photographic work is shown in New York, California and Chicago.

Films by Lynch (selection): The Beehive 2002 | The Urban Leprechaun 2003 | The Greek Myth 2004 | The Wash 2005 | Transposition of the Great Vessels 2005 (feature film)

Film by Schmitt (selection): Butoh Movie, Rain or Shine, Las Vegas 2000 | Nightingale 2002 | Awake and Sing 2003 (feature film) | The Wash 2005
