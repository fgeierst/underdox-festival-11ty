Ludwig's Tape

 

 

Claudia Siefen
Austria 2014/15
21 min – HD – engl. OV


Sc+P: Claudia Siefen – DoP: Sebastian Postl – E: Christine Dériaz – With Barry Currid (voice), Kevin Lutz

German premiere



           


 

The Austrian philosopher Ludwig Wittgenstein (1889-1951) and his passion for cinema, architecture, vegetarian food, scotch tape and changing the view from your hotel room windows. Last but not least his love for young men. We see a man and hear a voice, obviously irish. So who is it we are looking at and who is it we are listening to? LUDWIG'S TAPE got an itchy quality, an extraordinary rhythm in editing and a certain eroticism that is hard to describe. So we observe the man while doing ordinary things of daily life, and we are listening to passionate words not only of love, but also of loneliness. And the joy of it.

monday 12 oct 8.15 pm werkstattkino

Claudia Siefen born in 1972 in Cologne. Publishes essays, interviews und film reviews about Japanese and experimental cinema. She works as free lancer at the collection of the Austrian Film Museum Vienna.
