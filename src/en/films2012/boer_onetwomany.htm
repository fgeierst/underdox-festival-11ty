one, two, many


Manon de Boer – BE 2012 – 22 min.

C: Sébastien Koeppel – E: Manon de Boer – S: Bastien Gilson, Manon de Boer – M: Studium I. – L(élek)zem by István Matuz & Tre Canti Popolari #2 by Giacinto Scelsi –
P: Auguste Orts with dOCUMENTA (13)

16mm transferred on HD video – English

www.augusteorts.be

 

 

The film "one, two, many" is made up of three performances: a flute piece with continuous breathing, a spoken monologue, and a song by four singers in front of an audience. Starting from different audiovisual perspective, each section explores the existential space of the voice. Connecting the three performances are the central themes of the individual's body, listening to the other, and finding the right distance for multiple voices in a social space.

 

Manon de Boer, born in 1966 in Kodaicanal/India. She studied at the Akademie van Beeldende Kunsten in Rotterdam and at the Rijksakademie van Beeldende Kunsten in Amsterdam. Now she lives and works in Brussels.

Films (selection) Sylvia Kristel – Paris 2003 (UX’06) – Resonating Surfaces 2005 (UX’06) – Presto, Perfect Sound 2006 – Villes Saisies 2007 – Two Times 4'33" 2008 (UX’08)  – Attica 2008 (UX’08) – Dissonant 2010 – Think About Wood, Think About Metal 2011 – One, two, many 2012
