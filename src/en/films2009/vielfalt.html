The film highlights the variety of decorative concrete objects in public spaces and asks what will happen to all the elements and environments that were once set up to bring beauty and peace to our neighbourhood in the big cities and suburbs? These abstract formed parking barrieres or animal like beautified playgrounds are nowadays threatened to vanish or be destroyed by glas and wooden miscellanea. "We still don’t feel any loss yet, but..."

Rainer Knepperges, born in 1965 in Korschenbroich, Germany, was one of the founding members of "Filmclub 813", part of the legendary "Kölner Gruppe" and editor of the film magazine Gdinetmao. In 2005 he co-directed his first feature-length film Die Quereinsteigerinnen. He contributes to the film magazines Cargo, 24 and Sigi Götz Entertainment. He lives and works as filmmaker and writer in Cologne.

Katrin Leuthe, born 1967, lives as a teacher in Cologne. Exploring Diversity is the first collaboration of Knepperges & Leuthe.
