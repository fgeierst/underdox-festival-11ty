Emilie Serri

 

Montréal-based filmmaker and Fine Arts Concordia graduate Emilie Serri has worked mostly with celluloid. Since her beginnings in film, she has worked with composer Emmanuel Alberola, who also signs the music of various danse creations. Serri’s work is mainly concerned with capturing the tension that lies between the visible physical world and internal states.


 

So Certain I Was, I Was a Horse – Canada 2011
12 min – 16mm on HD – no dialogue
K: Philippe Léonard – V: Light Cone

An exploration of the body as ”living tableau”. Visual imprints on the physical body act as emerging fragments of interior states and intimate experiences buried in the unconscious.
